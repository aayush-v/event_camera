Event Datasets

Our dataset offers a comprehensive collection of real-world data recordings encompassing a diverse array of scenes and lighting conditions. In total, it comprises over 10 hours of data, ensuring a rich and varied resource for researchers and practitioners.

The data collection process spanned an extensive six-month period, with dedicated focus on different lighting conditions. Over six hours of data were meticulously recorded during bright daylight, providing valuable insights into daytime traffic dynamics. Additionally, an equivalent duration was devoted to twilight and post-sunset conditions, shedding light on the unique challenges posed by low-light environments.

To enhance the quality of the recorded sequences, each data point underwent a spatio-temporal filtering process. This technique, tailored to emphasize events originating from tangible objects while minimizing noise events, was particularly effective during nighttime data collection. It addressed sensitivity to reflections and pointed light sources from streets and vehicles, ensuring a clearer and more accurate representation of traffic scenarios. It is a simple yet effective filtering technique to de-noise the data, thus leaving scope for some more data preprocessing techniques to be performed.

The dataset encompasses a wide range of traffic participant classes, including vehicles such as cars, trucks, buses, trams, bicycles, and even non-motorized participants like pedestrians, cyclists, and wheelchair users. This diverse array of classes opens avenues for in-depth analysis and research aimed at enhancing the safety and efficiency of every traffic participant.

Annotations and Labels: We have over 1 hour of annotated data that includes over 200,000 areas of interest.

Researchers and practitioners can leverage this dataset for a multitude of applications, including but not limited to object detection, tracking, behavior analysis, and the development of advanced traffic monitoring systems.
